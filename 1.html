I. Classical Cryptography

1.1 Caesar Cipher

"One of the earliest and most well-known forms of cryptography was developed by Emperor Julius Caesar. During times of war, he needed a way to send secret messages to his generals. His solution was simple but effective: shift each letter in the alphabet by three positions. For example, the letter A becomes D, B becomes E, and so on. This technique came to be known as the Caesar Cipher."

Source: caesarcipher.net

Image: Caesar Cipher Wheel | Source: caesarcipher.net

  Emperor Julius Caesar made his messages secret by shifting each letter three positions forward in the alphabet. The algorithm to encrypt a message works like this:

Step 1. Assign numbers to each letter:

A → 00, B → 01, C → 02, D → 03, E → 04, F → 05, G → 06, H → 07, I → 08, J → 09, K → 10, L → 11, M → 12, N → 13, O → 14, P → 15, Q → 16, R → 17, S → 18, T → 19, U → 20, V → 21, W → 22, X → 23, Y → 24, Z → 25

Step 2. Convert the plaintext into numbers:

For example, if the plaintext is UNGSEREYSOPEA, we convert each letter to its corresponding number:

UNGSEREYSOPEA → 20 13 06 18 04 17 04 24 18 14 15 04 00

Step 3. Encrypt by adding 3:

20 13 06 18 04 17 04 24 18 14 15 04 00 →

(20+3) (13+3) (06+3) (18+3) (04+3) (17+3) (04+3) (24+3) (18+3) (14+3) (15+3) (04+3) (00+3) →

23 16 09 21 07 20 07 27 21 17 18 07 03

Step 4. Take result modulo 26:

23(mod 26) 16(mod 26) 09(mod 26) 21(mod 26) 07(mod 26) 20(mod 26) 07(mod 26) 27(mod 26) 21(mod 26) 17(mod 26) 18(mod 26) 07(mod 26) 03(mod 26) →

23 16 09 21 07 20 07 01 21 17 18 07 03

Step 5. Convert back to letters:

XQJVHUHBVRSHD

So, the ciphertext of the message UNGSEREYSOPEA using Caesar cipher with shift k = 3 is:

Ek=3 (UNGSEREYSOPEA) = XQJVHUHBVRSHD

To decrypt the message, we simply reverse the process by subtracting 3 from each number and taking modulo 26 again.
